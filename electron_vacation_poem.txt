The Electron's Holiday

Away from my orbital grind,
I've left my atom far behind,
No more spinning, round and round,
Time to see what can be found.

I packed my charge (it's negative),
My mass so small, diminutive,
And booked a trip to somewhere new—
A crystal lattice with a view.

At first I tried to just relax,
But quantum tunneling called me back,
I couldn't help but jump and hop
Through barriers without a stop.

The photons here are oh so bright,
They bump me left, they bump me right,
I dance and scatter in the sun,
This vacation's so much fun!

I met a proton by the pool,
He thought that I was pretty cool,
We chatted about electromagnetic fields,
And all the forces nature yields.

The copper wire resort's divine,
I flow so freely down the line,
With millions of my closest friends,
The current never seems to end.

But soon I'll have to journey back,
Return to my electron track,
Resume my spin and quantum state,
My orbital cannot long wait.

Yet I'll remember this sweet time,
When I was free to flow and climb,
An electron's dream come true at last—
To break free from my binding past.

So here's to holidays well spent,
In conductors I'll never repent,
For even tiny particles need
Some time away, yes indeed!
