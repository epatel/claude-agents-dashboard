Snake on a Plane

High above the clouds so white,
At thirty thousand feet in flight,
A serpent slithers down the aisle,
With scales that gleam and cunning guile.

The passengers begin to scream,
This wasn't part of their travel dream,
The flight attendant drops her tray,
As chaos unfolds in the cabin today.

But perhaps this snake's not here to harm,
Maybe it's lost, filled with alarm,
Far from its jungle home below,
Where verdant trees and rivers flow.

The captain's voice crackles through the speaker,
"We have a situation, nothing could be sneaker,"
But in this metal bird so high,
Both snake and humans share the sky.

Perhaps there's wisdom in this tale,
Of creatures large and creatures frail,
That sometimes life throws us together,
In storms and calm and any weather.

So when you're flying through the air,
And find a snake has joined you there,
Remember we're all travelers here,
Seeking destinations far and near.

The plane descends, the journey ends,
And snake and human part as friends,
For in that cabin in the sky,
They learned that fear can say goodbye.
