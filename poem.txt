The Garden Wanderer

A spiral house upon his back,
He carries home along the track,
Through morning dew and silver rain,
The snail moves slow but not in vain.

His gentle glide leaves trails of shine,
Like cursive script in nature's line,
Each curve and turn a story told,
Of patience worth its weight in gold.

No rush, no hurry marks his way,
He greets each leaf, each flower's spray,
With tentacles that reach and seek,
The world through senses soft and meek.

When danger comes, he pulls inside
His fortress where he safely hides,
Then ventures forth when all is clear,
To face the world without a fear.

Oh humble snail, teach us your grace,
To slow our hurried, frantic pace,
That life's sweet moments we might find
When we leave restless haste behind.
