The Sparrow's Song

Upon a branch so thin and high,
A sparrow sits beneath the sky,
With feathers brown and chest so bright,
A tiny beacon in morning light.

She chirps a melody so sweet,
As dawn and daybreak gently meet,
Her song carries on the breeze,
Through whispers of the swaying trees.

With nimble wings she darts and plays,
Through golden rays of springtime days,
No burden weighs upon her heart,
Each moment is a work of art.

Oh little bird with spirit free,
You teach us what we long to be—
To sing despite the world's great noise,
And find in simple things our joys.
