The Feathered Cyclist

With wings tucked tight against the breeze,
A robin rides through autumn trees,
Two tiny claws grip handlebars,
As bright as any morning stars.

She pedals past the morning dew,
Her red breast catching sunlight new,
No need for flight when wheels can roll
Across the path toward her goal.

The other birds look down in wonder,
"Why ride when you can soar like thunder?"
But she just chirps a joyful song,
And pedals merrily along.

Her basket filled with seeds and crumbs,
She rings her bell as evening comes,
A bird who chose a different way—
Adventure found in each new day.

Through puddles splashing, hills ascending,
This tale of courage never ending,
Reminds us all that dreams take flight
In unexpected ways so bright.

So when you see a bird on bike,
Remember this: we're all alike—
Sometimes the ground holds just as much
Magic as the sky's sweet touch.
